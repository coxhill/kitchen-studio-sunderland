Our Process

From Concept to Completion — Our Design Process

Our design-led process ensures your kitchen is right before a single unit is ordered — protecting your investment and eliminating costly surprises.

1 Discovery and Survey

We begin with a genuine discovery conversation — listening to how you use your kitchen, what frustrates you about your current one, and what your priorities are. We then visit your home to take a precise survey, capturing every dimension, structural consideration, and service position.

2 Design Development

Based on your survey and brief, our designers produce a scaled floor plan, detailed elevation drawings, and photorealistic 3D renders of your proposed kitchen. Changes at this stage are free; changes after ordering are not.

3 Material Selection

  • Cabinet door style, colour, and finish selection
  • Worktop material, colour, and edge profile choice
  • Hardware, sink, and tap selection
  • Splashback material and design confirmation
  • Lighting specification and control system

4 Ordering and Lead Time Management

We coordinate all supply chains — cabinetry manufacture, worktop templating and production, appliance delivery, and trade scheduling — so that everything arrives in the right sequence for an efficient installation.

5 Installation

  • Full strip-out and preparation including waste removal
  • First-fix electrical and plumbing adjustments
  • Precision unit installation on levelled adjustable legs
  • Worktop delivery, fitting, and full sealing
  • Appliance connection and commissioning
  • Splashback and all finishing details

Our installation standard: All door and drawer gaps consistent to 2–3mm. All worktop joints flush to 0.5mm. All sealant lines continuous. All plumbing tested under load. Every snagging item resolved before sign-off.

How to Choose

Choosing the Right Kitchen Showroom in Sunderland

Not all kitchen showrooms deliver the same standard of design expertise, product quality, or installation professionalism. Knowing what to look for protects your investment.

Questions to Ask Any Showroom

  • Where is the cabinetry manufactured — UK, Europe, or elsewhere?
  • What is the carcass board thickness in your standard specification?
  • Is the design consultation genuinely free with no obligation?
  • Can you provide references from recent comparable projects?
  • What does the workmanship guarantee cover and for how long?
  • Is waste removal and trade coordination included in the price?

Red Flags to Watch For

  • No written quotation — verbal estimates only
  • Demanding more than 25% deposit before ordering
  • Unable to provide recent customer references
  • No mention of workmanship guarantee
  • Carcass board under 18mm in standard specification
  • Vague scope of works — no itemised specification

Kitchen Studio Sunderland provides a full written specification, dedicated project manager, verified customer references, and a minimum 2-year workmanship guarantee on every project.
